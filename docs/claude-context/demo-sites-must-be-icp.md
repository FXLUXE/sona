The ICP gate is a **product-wide rule**, not just a demo convention. It ALWAYS applies: (a) demo/eval corpora, (b) the live app's prospect-finder/outreach (only surface ICP businesses, never chains/franchises), and (c) the dashboards/onboarding built for them — integrated into BOTH front end and back end of the app. Targets and corpora must be **small, independent, local UK businesses** — the actual buyers of Sona — NOT national chains, franchises, or big brands. Examples of what to AVOID (mistakes already made): Protyre, Whitegates, Mr Electric, PureGym, David Lloyd, Everyone Active, Village Gym, The Accountancy Partnership, Plumbingforce.

**Why:** A big company will never buy a small-business receptionist chatbot, so demoing one is meaningless to the ideal customer and skews the eval away from the real sites prospects actually have (smaller, thinner, often Wix/Squarespace/JS-built). The whole product only matters if it works for the sellable-to business.

**How to apply:** For every vertical, now and future, pick a genuinely independent local business (one or few locations, owner-run feel) and verify it is NOT a chain/franchise/national brand BEFORE ingesting. If a small site is thin/JS-built, that's the real test — use the render-fallback crawler, don't substitute a big brand for convenience.

**STRICT GATE (Daniel's words: "this is a strict gate to follow") — a site is only acceptable if ALL pass:**
1. Independent / owner-run; ONE location or a few local branches only.
2. NOT a chain, franchise, group, PLC, or national brand; no "branches across the UK" / nationwide-coverage claims.
3. NOT a directory / marketplace / aggregator / comparison site (Checkatrade, Yell, Fresha, Treatwell, Bark, etc.).
4. Has its own real small-business website (any platform, incl. Wix/Squarespace).
5. Would plausibly PAY for a small-business receptionist chatbot — i.e. an actual sellable-to prospect.
REJECT on any red flag: market-share/PLC/investor/careers-heavy corporate sites, "voted UK's #1", hundreds of staff, multi-region/national footprint. When unsure, reject and pick a smaller business.
